## Concept Goals
- Hero piece introducing PxWallet as the premium self-custody experience for the Px community.
- Make "encrypted local vault + cross-chain derivation" feel like discovering a hidden power-up in a retro game.
- Subtly teach: You control your keys. On-chain entropy + WharfKit = trustless power. No centralized risk.
- Drive initial interest / waitlist / beta signups.

## Target Audience
- Existing WAX users frustrated with seed phrases or multi-wallet chaos
- Pixal holders & Hot or Not players looking for seamless self-custody
- Newcomers who want the "fun retro pixel" vibe without sacrificing security

## Core Narrative Arc (30-45s)
1. **Hook (0-5s)**: Retro pixel character frantically losing seed phrases in classic game-over style. Screen shatters. "Never again."
2. **The Reveal (5-15s)**: Sleek glassmorphic PxWallet vault opens with satisfying pixel snap + subtle CRT glow. Master password derivation magic shown beautifully (no raw keys ever visible).
3. **The Power-Up (15-30s)**: Cross-chain key derivation visualized as elegant branching pixel tree (WAX → EVM → Solana). One master seed → god-mode control everywhere. WharfKit signing delight.
4. **Verifiable Flex (30-40s)**: Quick on-chain entropy or AtomicAssets Pixal view inside the wallet. "Your assets. Verifiable. Yours."
5. **CTA & End Card (40s+)**: "Press Start to take back control." Link to PxWallet. End card with Px branding + "Built on WAX — Self-custody is the new high score."

## Key Educational Delight Moment
The moment the vault opens and the derivation tree blooms — paired with a subtle TX or local encryption visual. Viewers feel the "I get it now" power without technical overwhelm.

## Success Metrics (Future)
- High completion rate on Reels/YouTube
- Click-through to PxWallet landing or waitlist
- Community remixes / quotes of the "god-mode" line
- Positive sentiment around self-custody education
